Quiet Beginnings

Not sure what's "wrong" with me but as long as I can remember I've had a profound and abiding love for "non-musical" sounds. My mom tells me when I was little I would "sing" along with the hum of the refridgerator and flush the toilet over and over again and dance around to the resulting noise.

Bigger kicks, Louder Sounds

A little later when I had some money of my own, I could often be found at the Streetside and the corner of Grand and Arsenal. It was during that time I picked up Public Enemy cassette "It Takes A Nation Of Millions To Hold Us Back." The whole thing was chock full of screaching sirens, breaking glass and deep rumbling bass. I was in love instantly.

So Raw, So Pure

For most of my adolescence the hunt for weird noise being used in popular music was a consuming and fruitless endeavor. I would often find myself listening to the squarest music for the little bit at the end of the track where the band gets a little loose and freaky. This typically would occur right before the track would fade out so essentially I would be listening to 4 minute pop songs for aproximately 5 seconds of sound I was interested in. But then file-sharing happened and I discovered The Velvet Underground, Throbbing Gristle and Merzbow. I haven't looked back since.
